FAQ

Common Questions

Everything you need to know before your first transaction.

What happens if the seller doesn't deliver?
‚ñº
If the seller fails to deliver within the agreed timeframe, the buyer can raise a dispute. Our mediators will review the situation and, if the seller is found to be in breach of the agreement, the funds will be fully refunded to the buyer. TrustBridge never releases funds without delivery confirmation or buyer approval.
What happens if the buyer refuses to approve?
‚ñº
If the buyer does not take any action during the inspection period, funds automatically release to the seller once the inspection window expires. If the buyer opens a dispute, our licensed mediators review all evidence. If the goods or services were delivered as agreed, TrustBridge will release funds to the seller.
How do I know TrustBridge won't just keep my money?
‚ñº
TrustBridge is a fully licensed and regulated escrow service. All client funds are held in segregated, FDIC-insured accounts entirely separate from our operational accounts. This means we legally cannot use client funds for any purpose other than the intended transaction. Our licensing and compliance documentation is available on request.
Can I cancel a transaction once it's started?
‚ñº
Yes ‚Äî either party can request a cancellation before the seller marks delivery as complete. Both parties must agree to the cancellation. If both agree, funds are returned to the buyer in full (minus any non-refundable payment processing fees). After delivery is marked, the normal inspection and dispute process applies.
How long does the whole process take?
‚ñº
The timeline depends entirely on the delivery method and inspection period both parties agree on. A domain name transfer might complete in 24 hours. A business acquisition with a 30-day due diligence period could take over a month. TrustBridge doesn't impose any time limits beyond what both parties set.
Is TrustBridge available internationally?
‚ñº
Yes. TrustBridge supports transactions in 180+ countries. International wire transfers and USDC cryptocurrency are the most popular cross-border payment methods. Note that certain high-risk jurisdictions may be restricted due to regulatory requirements. Contact support if you're unsure about your country.
What is USDC and why do you use it for crypto payments?
‚ñº
USDC (USD Coin) is a stablecoin ‚Äî a cryptocurrency whose value is pegged 1:1 to the US Dollar. Unlike Bitcoin or Ethereum, its value doesn't fluctuate. We use USDC on the Solana network because transactions are near-instant (under 1 second) and fees are negligible (fractions of a cent). This makes it ideal for fast, predictable cross-border payments.
